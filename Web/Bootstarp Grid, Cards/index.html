Autumn is the season of the year between summer and winter

Autumn

Autumn, also known as fall, typically begins around September 22nd or 23rd in the Northern Hemisphere. The word "autumn" is believed to have originated from the Latin word "autumnus." It's the season associated with the harvest of crops, making it a critical time for agriculture. In many cultures, autumn is celebrated with various festivals and holidays, such as Halloween and Thanksgiving. Animals, such as birds and monarch butterflies, migrate during the fall to find warmer climates.

Weather

Autumn weather is characterized by gradually decreasing temperatures as summer transitions into winter. It's the season with shorter days and longer nights. In some regions, autumn is known for its unpredictable weather, including occasional cold fronts, rain, and even the first snowfalls of the season. Weather patterns in autumn can vary significantly depending on the geographical location, with some areas experiencing mild, pleasant temperatures and others witnessing more extreme fluctuations.

Climate Changes

The changing angle of the Earth's axis and its orbit around the sun cause the shift in seasons, including the arrival of autumn. Autumn is often associated with lower humidity levels, making the air feel crisper and drier. In many temperate climates, trees shed their leaves during autumn as a strategy to conserve water and energy in preparation for the winter months. The transition to autumn weather is a critical trigger for many plants and animals to prepare for hibernation, dormancy, or migration.
